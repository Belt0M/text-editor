Adapted Reading Passage
Artificial intelligence, or AI, is a really cool technlogy that can help teachers in the classroom. There are many ways that AI can be used to make learning more fun and interesting for students. One way is by using a tool called Canva's "text-to-image" feature. With this tool, teachers can tell the computer what they want to see in a picture, and then the computer will create it. This can be really helpful for English Language Learners, or ELL students, because they can use images to help them learn new words and ideas.
Another way that AI can be used in the classroom is through online writing tools like Quill and Duolingo. These tools use something called adaptive learning, which means that they can figure out what students need help with and give them activities to practice those skills. It's like having a personal tutor!
AI can also be used to help students with their pronunciation. There are websites like English Central and Speakable that can listen to students speak and give them feedback on how to improve. This can be really helpful for ELL students who are learning to speak English.
Even though AI can be really helpful, there are some things we need to be careful about. For example, AI doesn't always give us the right information. It's important to double-check what we learn from AI and make sure it's correct. Also, AI doesn't have feelings or a moral compass like humans do. That means we need to be careful about how we use AI and make sure it's being used in a good way.
In conclusion, AI is a really cool technology that can help teachers in the classroom. It can help us learn new words and ideas, practice our writing and speaking skills, and get feedback on our pronunciation. But we also need to be careful and make sure we use AI in a responsible way.
Summary
  • AI can help teachers make learning more fun and interesting for students
  • AI tools like Quill and Duolingo use adaptive learning to personalize student activities
  • We need to be careful about using AI and double-check its information for accuracy
